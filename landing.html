SchemaGuard

The automated bouncer protecting your software from AI hallucinations.

What is SchemaGuard?

Imagine you hire a brilliant but slightly unpredictable assistant (the AI). You ask them to fill out a strictly formatted spreadsheet with numbers and dates. Sometimes they do it perfectly. Other times, they write a long paragraph instead of a simple number, spell their name backward, or completely forget a column.

When this happens in software, the entire program crashes. SchemaGuard is the supervisor standing perfectly between that AI and your app.

If the AI gives the wrong format, SchemaGuard catches it before it breaks anything, taps the AI on the shoulder, shows it exactly what it did wrong, and forces it to try again until it's perfect.

How It Works Behind the Scenes

1. Coercion Mode: The "Auto-Fixer"
Sometimes the AI makes a tiny, silly mistake. It might write the number "100" wrapped in quotes (making it a text string) instead of a raw number 100.

Normally, this crashes your app. But in Coercion Mode, SchemaGuard is smart enough to say, "I know what you meant," automatically converts the text into a real number behind the scenes, and safely hands it to your application without needing to waste time or money asking the AI again.
2. Auto-Retry Engine: The "Try Again"
If the AI completely forgets a required field (like a customer's total price), SchemaGuard automatically intercepts the failure, grabs the exact error (e.g., "Missing required field: Total Price"), injects it back into a new prompt, and tells the AI: "You failed. Fix this specific missing field."

It loops this until the AI produces perfectly formatted data.
3. The Circuit Breaker: Preventing Runaway Costs
AI models cost money every time you ask them a question. If an AI is severely confused and gets stuck in an infinite loop of failing, your credit card bill would explode in seconds.

Our Circuit Breaker acts as an emergency stop button. If the AI fails 5 times in a row, the circuit "trips" and locks the AI out temporarily. It stops spending your money, instantly throws an error to your developers, and protects your infrastructure from cascading token waste.

How To Use It (Quickstart)

1

Define the Rules

Tell SchemaGuard exactly what format you expect the AI to return using a standard JSON Schema.

const schema = {
  "type": "object",
  "properties": { 
     "userId": { "type": "integer" } 
  },
  "required": ["userId"]
};
2

Ask the AI

Normally, the AI might give you a conversational, broken response wrapped in markdown text blocks rather than pure code.

# The AI responds with:
"Sure! I'd love to help you with that. Here is your user ID!"
```json
{"userId": "12345"} 
```
"Hope that helps!"
3

Run It Through SchemaGuard

Offline Alpha Validation

Pass the AI's messy output directly through our Python SDK. SchemaGuard strips out the conversational text, finds the data block, and runs the rigorous checks.

from schemaguard import LocalValidator

# Load the bouncer
validator = LocalValidator(schema)

# Check the AI's homework
is_valid, clean_data, errors = validator.validate(ai_response)

if is_valid:
    print("Success! Data is bulletproof:", clean_data)
else:
    print("Hold up. Errors found:", errors)
